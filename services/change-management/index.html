Human-Centered Change Approach

Change management works best as a practice, not a project. Like jazz improvisation, it requires strong fundamentals, deep listening, and responsive adaptation.

1

Read the Room

Understand organizational dynamics, informal power structures, and the "background noise" that influences how change really happens.

2

Find Natural Champions

Identify teams with problems your change can solve. Let them become advocates through success, not mandates.

3

Build Coalition

Create change through conversation, not presentation. Build genuine buy-in across stakeholder groups.

4

Sustain Momentum

Celebrate small wins, address concerns quickly, and adapt approach based on what's actually working.
