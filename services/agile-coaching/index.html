Agile as Practice, Not Process

Beyond Methodology Adherence

Too many agile transformations fail because they focus on perfect process execution rather than delivering value. After coaching teams at Fortune 500 companies like USAA and Progressive, I've learned that successful agile adoption happens when teams understand the principles, not just the practices.

My approach combines traditional agile methodologies with design thinking and lean principles, creating frameworks that work for your specific context and culture.

"Outcomes and relationships trump process and adherence. The goal isn't perfect scrum—it's delivering value to customers and creating sustainable team practices."

Transformation Success: USAA

The Challenge

USAA needed to coordinate agile practices across 50+ development teams while maintaining quality and compliance requirements for financial services.

The Approach

Implemented SAFe framework with customizations for USAA's unique context. Focused on building agile champions within teams rather than imposing top-down transformation.

The Impact

Teams achieved predictable delivery cadence, improved cross-team collaboration, and significantly reduced time-to-market for new features while maintaining compliance standards.
