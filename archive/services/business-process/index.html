Design Philosophy

People-First Process Design

Too often, business process design starts with technology capabilities or theoretical efficiency models. Better approach: start with how people actually work, then design processes that amplify their strengths while minimizing friction.

After 15+ years transforming workflows at companies like Progressive Insurance and USAA, I've learned that sustainable process improvement happens when you solve human problems, not just business problems.

"Outcomes and relationships trump process and adherence. The goal isn't perfect methodology—it's making things work better for actual people."

Common Process Design Failures

Technology-First Thinking

Processes designed around system capabilities rather than user needs, leading to workarounds and shadow processes.

Impact: 40% lower adoption rates, increased training costs

Over-Optimization

Pursuit of theoretical efficiency that ignores human factors like cognitive load, error recovery, and edge cases.

Impact: Brittle processes that break under real-world conditions

Compliance Theater

Processes designed to satisfy auditors rather than enable productive work, creating bureaucratic overhead.

Impact: Decreased productivity, increased resistance to change

One-Size-Fits-All

Standardized processes that don't account for contextual differences across teams, roles, or business units.

Impact: Poor fit leads to workarounds and inconsistent outcomes

Human-Centered Process Design

Workflow Analysis

Understanding how work actually happens vs. how it's supposed to happen.

  • Observational research and user interviews
  • Shadow process documentation
  • Pain point and friction analysis
  • Stakeholder journey mapping

Collaborative Design

Co-creating solutions with the people who will use them daily.

  • Cross-functional design workshops
  • Rapid prototyping and testing
  • Iterative refinement based on feedback
  • Change champion development

Implementation Strategy

Sustainable rollout that builds adoption through value demonstration.

  • Pilot program design and execution
  • Training and support systems
  • Performance metrics and feedback loops
  • Continuous improvement frameworks

Case Study: Progressive Insurance Legal Review Process

The Challenge

State filing processes required legal review coordination across multiple departments, creating bottlenecks and compliance risks.

The Approach

Designed collaborative workflow that integrated legal review into development process rather than treating it as separate approval gate.

The Innovation

Created early-warning system that flagged potential legal issues during content creation, preventing downstream bottlenecks.

Review Cycle Time -60%
Compliance Issues -75%
Process Satisfaction +85%
Rework Reduction -50%

Process Design Methodology

1

Discovery & Analysis

Map current state workflows, identify pain points, and understand stakeholder needs through observation and interviews.

Tools: Journey mapping, process mining, stakeholder interviews
2

Design & Prototype

Co-create improved workflows with end users, focusing on reducing friction and enabling better outcomes.

Tools: Collaborative workshops, service blueprints, low-fi prototypes
3

Pilot & Refine

Test new processes with pilot groups, gather feedback, and iterate based on real-world usage patterns.

Tools: A/B testing, feedback collection, performance metrics
4

Scale & Sustain

Roll out proven processes with proper training, support systems, and continuous improvement mechanisms.

Tools: Change management, training design, feedback loops
